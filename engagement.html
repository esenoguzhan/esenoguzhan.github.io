The Legal Footing

How this is set up

01

A registered business

I operate as a registered sole proprietorship in Türkiye and invoice as an independent supplier — a recognized entity with its own tax identity, not an informal freelance arrangement.

02

A recognized service-export framework

Turkish law explicitly names engineering among the services whose export it incentivizes: from the 2026 tax period, 100% of this income is deductible from my income-tax base. That favorable treatment on my side is precisely what lets me offer a competitive rate on a structure the state actively supports.

Income Tax Law 89/13

03

A genuine independent engagement

I carry all of my own tax and social-security obligations in Türkiye. You engage a supplier, not an employee — no employment relationship and no employer duties on your side.

04

Contract, confidentiality, IP

Scope, deliverables, confidentiality (NDA), and intellectual-property transfer are set out in a written services agreement. Rights to the delivered work pass to you by contract.

FAQ

Common questions

How is this cheaper than hiring locally?

You pay only for delivered work — no employer contributions, benefits, leave, or severance, and no recruitment or fixed overhead. The rate reflects the work, not the full cost of employment.

Is this a stable, legitimate arrangement?

Yes. I operate as a registered business in Türkiye under a service-export framework that Turkish law explicitly supports, with all of my tax obligations handled on my side.

Are we taking on an employee?

No. You engage an independent supplier under a written services contract — no employment relationship and no employer obligations.

Who owns the work?

You do. Intellectual-property transfer is defined in the contract, and rights to all deliverables pass to you.

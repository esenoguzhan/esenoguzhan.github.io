Remote Engineering Services (Türkiye → Global)

Work with me remotely while I invoice you legally from Türkiye — optimized for cost, compliance, and speed.

Summary

I’m setting up a personal company in Türkiye so I can invoice international clients while working remotely as an engineer. Thanks to tech‑sector tax incentives in Türkiye, I can offer more competitive rates without compromising on quality or compliance.

What this means for you

  • Simple contracting: You hire me as an independent contractor; I deliver engineering work remotely.
  • Proper invoices & compliance: You receive a formal invoice from my Turkish company — suitable for your accounting and audits.
  • Cost advantage: Turkish R&D/tech incentives reduce my tax burden, allowing more competitive pricing for you.
  • Global collaboration: Async + real‑time support (EU time zones) with clear SLAs.
  • No employer overhead: You do not need to handle health insurance, benefits, or additional employment costs — everything is included in my rate.

Note: This page describes my plan and operating model; it is not legal or tax advice.

Engagement models

  • Fixed‑scope project — clear deliverables, milestones, and acceptance tests.
  • Time & materials — flexible scope with weekly reporting.
  • Retainer — ongoing support (e.g., 40 h/month) with priority response.

Typical workflow

  1. Discovery call → requirements, interfaces, constraints.
  2. Proposal → scope, timeline, estimate, and IP/NDAs if needed.
  3. Contract + PO → invoicing entity: my Turkish personal company.
  4. Execution → frequent demos, git access, CI builds.
  5. Delivery → documentation, handover, and support window.

Invoicing & compliance

  • Entity: Personal company registered in Türkiye (engineering/tech services).
  • Currency: EUR or USD (mutually agreed). TRY available if preferred.
  • Invoice format: Compliant with Turkish regulations; includes tax IDs and service description.
  • Payment methods: Bank transfer (SEPA/SWIFT). Other methods on request.
  • IP & confidentiality: Custom clauses or your standard MSA/NDA are welcome.

I handle Turkish tax filings on my side. You book the invoice as a cross‑border service expense per your local rules.

Pricing & tax advantage

Because high‑tech personal companies in Türkiye may benefit from reduced effective taxation (subject to eligibility and program terms), I can:

  • Offer lower day‑rates vs. typical EU contractors of similar seniority.
  • Keep pricing stable across longer engagements.
  • Reinvest savings into tooling, testing, and QA (benefiting your project).

Exact pricing depends on scope and eligibility of incentives at the time of engagement.

Frequently asked questions

Q: Can my company pay you as a contractor? Yes. I issue a compliant invoice from Türkiye; your team records it as a service expense.

Q: Will we have a VAT issue? Cross‑border B2B services typically place VAT at the customer’s location under reverse‑charge rules in many jurisdictions. We’ll reflect the correct wording on the invoice. (Confirm with your accounting.)

Q: Time zones and availability? I’m based in Türkiye (CET/CEST) and can align with EU teams; limited overlap with US/Asia can be arranged.

Q: Can we sign our standard NDA/MSA? Absolutely.

Q: Do you work on‑site? Primarily remote; short on‑site visits are possible by arrangement.

Q: What about employee benefits or insurances? You don’t need to worry about health insurance, social security, or employee benefits — these are handled on my side as a self‑employed contractor.
